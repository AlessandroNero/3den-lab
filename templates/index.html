How DNA is Transformed into Music:

The process of converting DNA sequences into music is fascinating and involves a translation of genetic material into musical elements.

1. Binary to DNA

In the first step, binary data (0s and 1s) is converted into DNA. This is done by mapping pairs of bits to the four DNA bases: Adenine (A), Cytosine (C), Guanine (G), and Thymine (T). For example, "00" becomes A, "01" becomes C, "10" becomes G, and "11" becomes T.

2. DNA to Music

The DNA sequence is then mapped into musical notes. Each of the four bases (A, C, G, T) is associated with a musical note: - A = Do - C = Re - G = Mi - T = Fa.

These notes are then used to create a musical melody. Each sequence of DNA, once translated, forms the structure for the music, where each base determines the pitch.

3. MIDI to DNA

In the reverse process, MIDI data representing musical notes can be converted back to DNA. This is done by reading specific notes and mapping them back to the DNA bases.
